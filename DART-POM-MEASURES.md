# River Dart — proposed measures for the South Devon Programme of Measures (cycle 4 RBMP)

*Friends of the Dart submission to the EA catchment-partner engagement (Reasons & Measures tool,
Devon South), 14 June 2026. Each measure is mapped to the workbook's "2. Add New Measures" columns
and the controlled categories (sheet 3), with the supporting evidence drawn from River Hub
(riverhub.vercel.app) — Environment Agency EDM spill returns + Friends of the Dart citizen-science
sampling. Figures are from the monitored record (EDM 2021–2024; sampling to date).*

## How our data maps to the consultation

The draft Dart measures address **diffuse agriculture** (nutrients/soil/livestock), **continuous
sewage discharge**, and **abstraction/flow**. Two things are largely **absent** that our data
evidences strongly:

1. **Intermittent storm-overflow discharges (dry-weather spilling).** No Dart water body has a
   measure under category **WQA04 "Improve sewerage drainage systems (including intermittent
   discharges)"** — yet our data shows heavy *dry-weather* spilling (untreated sewage discharged
   when no rainfall justifies it) at named assets, several on already-failing water bodies.
2. **Evidence for "suspect data" / unassigned-RNAG elements.** Several Dart elements have no
   RNAG/measure (Dart *Fish* = "suspect data"; Dart Tidal, Mardle, Dean Burn, Wash *Fish* blank).
   The FAQ says these need monitoring/investigation — our citizen dataset is exactly that evidence.

A note on scope, stated honestly: WFD ecological classification here turns on **macrophytes/
phytobenthos, fish and phosphate**, not bacteria. Our **E. coli** data does not itself drive those
classifications, but it is a **tracer of the sewage/faecal inputs** behind the point-source and
diffuse RNAGs, and it underpins the **protected-area / bathing** case. Dry-weather sewage spills
deliver organic load, solids and nutrients — directly relevant to the macrophyte/phytobenthos and
phosphate failures.

## Part B — Proposed new measures (for "2. Add New Measures")

Each block = the sheet-2 fields. Tier codes from sheet 3; Aims from sheet 6; sectors from sheet 8.

### B1. Eliminate dry-weather spilling — Rattery STW, Bidwell Brook
- **Waterbody:** Bidwell Brook — `GB108046005160`
- **Tier 1 / Tier 2:** *4: Improve sewerage drainage systems (including intermittent discharges)* /
  **WQA04.01** (review/vary the discharge); supported by **WQA01.03** (identify cause of RNAG)
- **Element(s):** Macrophytes and Phytobenthos Combined · **RNAG:** Point source – sewage discharge (intermittent)
- **Aim:** Reverse deterioration / Improvement to classification status
- **Delivery / Lead:** Water Industry – Waste water treatment (South West Water; WINEP)
- **Evidence:** Rattery STW — highest dry-weather spill count of any Dart asset (~1,700+ dry spills,
  record); Bidwell Brook E. coli median 910; Dartington Beach 1,000 d/s. Dry spilling is
  presumptively non-compliant (UWWTR 1994 Reg 4(4)).

### B2. Address pumping-station storm spills to the Dart estuary
- **Waterbody:** Dart (Tidal) — `GB108046005060`
- **Tier 1 / Tier 2:** **WQA04.01** (intermittent discharges)
- **Element(s):** Macrophytes and Phytobenthos · **RNAG:** Point source – sewage discharge (intermittent)
- **Aim:** Prevention of deterioration · also *Contributes to a protected area objective* (bathing)
- **Lead:** Water Industry – Waste water treatment (SWW)
- **Evidence:** Warfleet Creek, Ferry Boat, Lower Ferry and Mill Creek pumping stations all repeat
  dry-weather spillers to the estuary; Steamer Quay (Totnes) median **E. coli 1,600** (n=97) at a
  popular access point.

### B3. Storm-overflow improvement / permit enforcement — Broadhempston STW, Hems
- **Waterbody:** Hems – Lower `GB108046005430` (works in Hems – Upper `GB108046005230`)
- **Tier 1 / Tier 2:** **WQA04.01** + **WQA03.01** (review/vary/enforce discharge permit)
- **Element(s):** Fish; Phosphate · **RNAG:** Point source – sewage discharge (intermittent)
- **Aim:** Reverse deterioration · **Lead:** Water Industry – Waste water treatment
- **Evidence:** Broadhempston STW repeat dry-weather spiller; d/s sampling E. coli 800.

### B4. Resolve "suspect data" / unassigned Fish classifications with citizen monitoring
- **Waterbodies:** Dart `GB108046008350` (Fish = *suspect data*); Dart (Tidal) `GB108046005060`;
  Mardle `GB108046005220`; Dean Burn `GB108046005190`; Wash `GB108046005080` (Fish blank/no RNAG)
- **Tier 1 / Tier 2:** *1: Investigation* — **WQA01.02** (improve confidence there is a genuine
  problem) and **WQA01.03** (identify the cause of RNAGs)
- **Aim:** Improvement to classification status/potential
- **Delivery / Lead:** Catchment partnership (Friends of the Dart) citizen-science monitoring
- **Evidence:** FotD holds an open, structured dataset (130 sites, 2,500+ results + per-asset EDM
  dry-spill classification) it will contribute to resolve these. Example: **Mardle** has no RNAG yet
  its sampling site reads median **E. coli 1,250** (n=16) — prima facie a genuine problem warranting
  investigation, not "no measure".

### B5. Investigate extreme bacterial hotspots in the upper Dart / Hems tributaries
- **Waterbody:** to be confirmed by source-tracing (upper Dart / Hems sub-catchment tributaries —
  Ambrook, Torbryan Stream, Tripe's Copse) — *these sampling sites are not yet assigned to a WFD
  water body; assignment is itself a recommended action*
- **Tier 1 / Tier 2:** **WQA01.03** (identify cause); likely **WQA06.x** (private STW / septic tanks)
  and **WQA03.03** (targeted farm inspections) once source is found
- **Element(s):** supports phosphate/macrophyte RNAGs; bacterial public-health concern
- **Aim:** Reverse deterioration / Prevent deterioration
- **Evidence:** median **E. coli 3,750 (Ambrook), 3,500 (Tripe's Copse), 3,300 (Torbryan Stream)** —
  far above other sites — pointing to concentrated faecal sources (failing septic tanks/farmyard
  run-off) needing source-tracing.

### B6. Designated bathing-water / protected-area objective at popular swim & estuary sites
- **Waterbodies:** Dart `GB108046008350` (Dartington/Staverton); Dart (Tidal) `GB108046005060`
  (Steamer Quay, Dittisham)
- **Tier 1 / Tier 2:** **WQA01.01** (investigations to determine appropriate objectives/standards)
- **Aim:** Contributes to a protected area objective
- **Delivery / Lead:** Catchment partnership + EA (bathing-water designation route)
- **Evidence:** sustained high E. coli at popular access points — Dartington Beach 1,000 (n=74),
  Steamer Quay 1,600 (n=97), Littlehempston 1,800 (n=13) — supports designation, which would compel
  monitoring and improvement.

## Part C — Caveats & data provenance (for assurance)
- **Dry spills:** EDM events with ≤0.25 mm rainfall on the spill day and the preceding day at the
  asset's nearest EA gauge (the EA/Ofwat definition); short (<15 min) single-interval events
  excluded as monitor noise. Method: `DRY-SPILL-METHOD.md` (versioned). We test the rainfall limb
  only — a dry spill is *presumptively* non-compliant, an investigate-signal.
- **E. coli:** medians over ≥10 samples; freshwater unless noted tidal.
- **Site→water-body links** are incomplete in our data (a known gap) — water-body attributions above
  are by sampling-site location and EDM outlet water body; the tributary hotspots (B5) need formal
  assignment.
- All evidence is reproducible from River Hub (per-asset dossiers, exportable) and ultimately from EA
  EDM returns + the FotD sampling record.

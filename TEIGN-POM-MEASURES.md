# River Teign — proposed measures for the South Devon Programme of Measures (cycle 4 RBMP)

*Friends of the River Teign submission to the EA catchment-partner engagement (Reasons & Measures
tool, Devon South), 15 June 2026. Companion to the Friends of the Dart submission
(`DART-POM-MEASURES.md`); same method, same workbook. Each measure maps to the workbook's
"2. Add New Measures" columns and controlled categories (sheet 3), with evidence drawn from River Hub
(the Teign instance) — Environment Agency EDM spill returns (2021–2024) and the EA WINEP (PR24/PR19)
action set. The Teign instance launched **spills-first**: there is **no citizen water-quality
sampling record yet**, so — unlike the Dart — this submission leads on spill frequency/duration and
WINEP commitments, and proposes a monitoring measure that the new FoRT sampling programme will feed.*

## How our data maps to the consultation

The draft Teign measures (81 RNAG rows across ~20 water bodies) cluster into: **diffuse agriculture**
(phosphate/sediment, livestock & soil), **continuous sewage discharge** (phosphate permit reviews at
STWs), **fish-passage / morphology** (Parke Weir, Teign barrier assessment), and **abandoned
metal mines** (cadmium/copper/lead/zinc on Beadon Brook and the Lower Teign). As with the Dart, two
things are **under-represented** that our data evidences strongly:

1. **Intermittent storm-overflow discharge (dry-weather spilling).** Only **one** Teign water body —
   **Lower Bovey `GB108046008470`** — carries a "sewage discharge (intermittent)" RNAG (NP_M414).
   Yet the EDM record shows enormous storm-overflow discharge volumes on water bodies whose only
   sewage RNAG is *continuous* discharge, or which have no sewage measure at all.
2. **Monitoring evidence on "suspect data" / unassigned elements.** Several Teign elements are flagged
   "Moderate (2025 – Det.)" or carry "Investigation to determine Reasons" (Reedy Brook Fish, Becka
   Brook Fish, Sowton Brook invertebrates). FoRT's forthcoming sampling is exactly that evidence.

Scope, stated honestly (as for the Dart): WFD ecological status here turns on macrophytes/
phytobenthos, fish and phosphate, not bacteria. Storm-overflow spills deliver organic load, solids
and nutrients — directly relevant to the macrophyte/phytobenthos and phosphate failures that
dominate the Teign list — and are a tracer of the sewage inputs behind the point-source RNAGs.

## Part A — Support for existing draft measures (column Q responses, with evidence)

- **Lower Bovey — intermittent-discharge RNAG (NP_M414) + macrophyte permit review (NP_M413).**
  *Strongly agree.* Add evidence: the Lower Bovey overflows discharged **8,947 h across 658 counted
  spills (2021–24)** — Heathfield STW plus the Bovey Tracey CSO cluster (Riverside, Bovey Bridge,
  Ayliffe's Field, A382 Bypass Verge). WINEP already places a storm-overflow **improvement
  (EnvAct_IMP2, due 2030)** on Heathfield and **investigations (EnvAct_INV4, due 2027)** on the whole
  cluster — so the intermittent-discharge measure is corroborated by both the EDM record and SWW's own
  WINEP commitments. This is the template the other Teign water bodies should follow.
- **Teign (Lower) & Teign (Upper) — "Review Discharge Permits … Phosphate" (NP_M1062/1071 etc.).**
  *Agree — extend to intermittent discharge.* The Lower Teign carries **23,636 h** of storm spilling
  (Christow, Chudleigh, Trusham, Chudleigh Knighton, Teign Village STWs); the Upper Teign **5,707 h**
  (Greenacres, Chagford). The permit review should cover **storm-overflow (intermittent) discharge**,
  not only the continuous consent. WINEP corroborates: **Chudleigh STW** EnvAct_IMP2 + INV4;
  **Bridford STW** U_IMP1 (due 2030).
- **Aller Brook — "Review Discharge Permits … Phosphate" (NP_M803).** *Agree.* Compton Castle CSO
  alone spilled **6,554 h / 508 spills**; the water body totals **9,080 h**. WINEP places
  **EnvAct_INV4 + U_IMP4** on Compton Castle — the permit review should include the intermittent limb.
- **Reedy Brook — "Investigation to determine Reasons – Fish" (NP_M1249/M1250).** *Agree, and the
  investigation must include storm spilling.* The Reedy Brook Fish failure is flagged suspect/"to
  determine"; the brook receives **12,313 h of storm discharge from the single Dunsford STW outlet**
  — by far the largest single-outlet load in the catchment. WINEP already places **EnvAct_IMP2 +
  INV4** on Dunsford. The investigation should explicitly test the sewage-spill contribution.
- **Fish-passage measures (Parke Weir, Teign Barrier Assessment, Teign River Restoration; New_M269–
  M281).** *Agree — no change.* These address the morphology/barrier RNAGs that drive most Teign Fish
  failures and are well-founded.

## Part B — Proposed new measures (for "2. Add New Measures")

Each block = the sheet-2 fields. Tier codes from sheet 3; Aims from sheet 6; sectors from sheet 8.
Evidence is EA EDM counted spills/duration (2021–24) and the EA WINEP action set.

### B1. Eliminate dry-/storm-weather spilling — Moretonhampstead STW, Wray Brook
- **Waterbody:** Wray Brook — `GB108046008330`
- **Tier 1 / Tier 2:** *4: Improve sewerage drainage systems (including intermittent discharges)* /
  **WQA04.01** (review/vary the discharge); supported by **WQA01.03** (identify cause of RNAG)
- **Element(s):** Fish; Phosphate · **RNAG:** Point source – sewage discharge (intermittent)
- **Aim:** Reverse deterioration / Improvement to classification status
- **Delivery / Lead:** Water Industry – Waste water treatment (South West Water; WINEP)
- **Evidence:** Moretonhampstead STW (two outlets) discharged **~20,050 h across ~1,100 counted spills
  (2021–24)** — the highest of any Teign works; Wray Brook totals **25,685 h / 1,725 spills**, the
  most-spilled Teign water body. Wray Brook is Fish *Moderate* and Phosphate *Poor*, yet has **no
  intermittent-discharge measure and no WINEP storm-overflow improvement on Moretonhampstead** — the
  single clearest gap in the Teign programme.

### B2. Storm-overflow improvement / investigation scope — Dunsford STW, Reedy Brook
- **Waterbody:** Reedy Brook — `GB108046008590`
- **Tier 1 / Tier 2:** **WQA04.01** (intermittent discharges) + **WQA01.02** (improve confidence the
  problem is genuine — the Fish failure is "to determine")
- **Element(s):** Fish; Dissolved oxygen · **RNAG:** Point source – sewage discharge (intermittent)
- **Aim:** Reverse deterioration · **Lead:** Water Industry – Waste water treatment (SWW; WINEP)
- **Evidence:** the single Dunsford STW outlet discharged **12,313 h / 593 spills (2021–24)** into a
  small brook whose Fish element is failing with "suspect data". WINEP already commits EnvAct_IMP2
  (improvement, 2030) + INV4 (investigation, 2027) at Dunsford — this measure ties the existing Fish
  investigation (NP_M1249) to that storm-overflow work.

### B3. Intermittent-discharge permit review across the Lower Teign STW cluster
- **Waterbody:** Teign (Lower) — `GB108046008540`
- **Tier 1 / Tier 2:** **WQA04.01** + **WQA03.01** (review/vary/enforce discharge permit)
- **Element(s):** Macrophytes and Phytobenthos; Phosphate · **RNAG:** Point source – sewage discharge
  (intermittent), alongside the existing continuous-discharge RNAG
- **Aim:** Reverse deterioration · **Lead:** Water Industry – Waste water treatment
- **Evidence:** **23,636 h / 2,706 spills (2021–24)** across Christow (6,956 h), Chudleigh (7,896 h
  across two outlets), Trusham (3,210 h), Chudleigh Knighton (1,829 h) and Teign Village STWs. WINEP:
  Chudleigh EnvAct_IMP2 + INV4; Bridford U_IMP1.

### B4. Storm spills to Aller Brook — Compton Castle
- **Waterbody:** Aller Brook — `GB108046005340`
- **Tier 1 / Tier 2:** **WQA04.01** + **WQA03.01**
- **Element(s):** Phosphate · **RNAG:** Point source – sewage discharge (intermittent)
- **Aim:** Reverse deterioration · **Lead:** Water Industry – Waste water treatment (SWW; WINEP)
- **Evidence:** Compton Castle CSO **6,554 h / 508 spills**; water body **9,080 h / 979 spills**.
  WINEP places EnvAct_INV4 + U_IMP4 on Compton Castle — the existing phosphate permit review (NP_M803)
  should be extended to the intermittent limb.

### B5. Establish a citizen water-quality baseline (Friends of the River Teign)
- **Waterbodies:** Reedy Brook `GB108046008590`; Becka Brook `GB108046008310`; Sowton Brook
  `GB108046008560`; and downstream of the B1–B4 overflows (priority sampling sites)
- **Tier 1 / Tier 2:** *1: Investigation* — **WQA01.02** (improve confidence there is a genuine
  problem) and **WQA01.03** (identify the cause of RNAGs)
- **Element(s):** Fish; Macrophytes/Phytobenthos; Invertebrates (the "suspect/to-determine" elements)
- **Aim:** Improvement to classification status/potential
- **Delivery / Lead:** Catchment partnership (Friends of the River Teign) citizen-science monitoring
- **Evidence:** the Teign has **no citizen water-quality record yet** (River Hub Teign launched
  spills-first). FoRT is standing up a sampling programme on the same open platform and method as
  Friends of the Dart (130 sites, 2,500+ results). Several Teign Fish/invertebrate RNAGs are flagged
  "suspect data" / "Investigation to determine Reasons" — exactly the gap structured citizen sampling
  resolves. This measure commits FoRT to deliver that evidence and feed it to the EA.

### B6. Storm spilling to Liverton Brook — Summerhill SPS (Ilsington)
- **Waterbody:** Liverton Brook — `GB108046008440`
- **Tier 1 / Tier 2:** **WQA04.01** (intermittent discharges); supported by **WQA01.03**
- **Element(s):** Fish · **RNAG:** Point source – sewage discharge (intermittent)
- **Aim:** Prevent deterioration · **Lead:** Water Industry – Waste water treatment
- **Evidence:** the single Summerhill SPS outlet discharged **5,686 h / 358 spills (2021–24)** into
  Liverton Brook, whose Fish failure is currently attributed only to barriers/morphology (NP_M1015) —
  the sewage-spill contribution is unassessed and should be added to the investigation.

## Part C — Caveats & data provenance (for assurance)
- **Spills:** EA EDM Annual Return *counted spills (12/24-hour)* and *total annual spill duration*,
  summed 2021–2024, attributed to WFD water bodies by the EA's own `wfd_waterbody_id` on each outlet.
  These are the regulator's headline figures — conservative, not the precautionary view.
- **Dry-weather classification:** River Hub holds the per-event dry/wet classification for the Teign
  (same method as the Dart, `DRY-SPILL-METHOD.md`); this submission uses **total** EA counts rather
  than the dry-only subset, so the storm-overflow case is *understated*. Dry-spill figures can be
  added per overflow on request.
- **No bacterial data yet:** unlike the Dart, the Teign has no citizen E. coli record — hence measure
  B5. All Part A/B sewage evidence rests on EDM spill frequency/duration + WINEP, not bacteria.
- **WINEP:** EA Water Industry National Environment Programme (PR24), matched to Teign works/water
  bodies by name and WFD id — see `WINEP-DATA-RESEARCH.md`. Cited actions are SWW's own accepted
  ("Proceed") commitments.
- All evidence is reproducible from River Hub (Teign instance) and ultimately from EA EDM returns and
  the EA WINEP dataset.

# River Dart — priority spilling assets & regulatory measures

*Partner briefing. Derived from River Hub data, July 2026: EA storm-overflow (EDM) annual returns
2020–2025, live spill events 2026, and the PR24/PR19 WINEP national dataset. Figures are open EA data.*

## Headline
The Dart's biggest sewage-overflow problems are a handful of rural treatment works that have spilled
heavily for years and are **still spilling in 2026**. Critically, the **worst chronic spillers are
under EA *monitoring/investigation* only — not a committed *improvement* scheme**. Firm upgrade
obligations exist for just a few assets (Rattery, Dartmouth, Princetown, Broadhempston), and the two
largest are not due until **2030**.

## How to read this
- **Lifetime spills / hours** — summed EA annual returns 2020–2025 (regulator-verified totals).
- **2026** — spills recorded so far this year by the live EDM feed (evidence of *continuing* spills,
  not a full-year figure).
- **Regulatory status** — WINEP obligations. WINEP distinguishes **"improve"** (physically upgrade
  the asset) from **"monitor/investigate"** (measure/study it — no committed fix). A works can carry
  many monitoring actions and still have no obligation to actually reduce spills.
- SO and SSO are two outfalls of one works — combined here.

## 2. Regulatory measures — where a fix is actually mandated
Only these worst-spillers carry a WINEP **improvement** obligation (a required upgrade), with its
legal deadline:

| Works | Improvement driver | Legal deadline |
|---|---|---|
| **Rattery STW** | `U_IMP1` Urban Waste Water Regs | **13 May 2030** |
| **Dartmouth STW** | `U_IMP1` Urban Waste Water Regs | **13 May 2030** |
| **Princetown STW** | `U_IMP5` Urban Waste Water Regs | **31 Mar 2024** — past due; spilled 2× in 2026 |
| **Broadhempston STW** | `U_IMP6` Urban Waste Water Regs | 31 Mar 2023 |
| Lady Meadow STW (Widecombe) | `U_IMP7` Urban Waste Water Regs | 31 Mar 2028 |

**Everything else — including the five biggest chronic spillers (Kilbury, Harberton, Harbertonford,
Holne, Scorriton) — is covered only by monitoring/investigation drivers** (`U_MON3/4`, `U_INV2`,
`SW_MON` event-duration monitoring, `NERC_INV1` biodiversity), most with 2021–2022 completion dates.
The EA is measuring these assets; South West Water has committed to no scheme to reduce their spills.

## 3. The asks this supports
1. **Rattery STW** — worst dry-weather spiller, still spilling in 2026, improvement not due until
   **2030**: press for an earlier delivery date.
2. **Princetown STW** — its improvement (`U_IMP5`) was due **March 2024** yet it spilled again in
   2026: ask SWW/EA whether it was delivered and why spills continue.
3. **Kilbury, Harberton, Harbertonford, Holne** — the biggest chronic spillers with **no improvement
   obligation at all**: press for these to be added to the improvement programme, not left on
   monitoring.

## Driver glossary
`U_IMPn` = Urban Waste Water Regs *improvement* (upgrade) · `U_INV2` = UWWTR investigation ·
`U_MON2/3/4`, `SW_MON` = monitoring (event-duration/flow) · `NERC_INV1` = biodiversity-duty
investigation · `EE_IMP` = eel-regs improvement · `HD_INV`/`WFD_INV_MOD`/`WFD_IMP_MOD` = Habitats /
Water Framework Directive investigation & improvement · `INNS_INV` = invasive-species investigation.

## Caveats
- 2026 counts are partial-year (live feed) — evidence of continuing spills, not annual totals.
- WINEP links to an asset via its **water body/works**, so per-asset action *counts* are water-body
  context; the **named** improvement obligations above are the asset-specific facts.
- Rankings blend spill **frequency** and **duration**; both are shown so neither is hidden.

## 4. Dry-weather spills — the compliance hook
A **dry-weather spill** (no rainfall to excuse it) is presumptively non-compliant — the strongest
regulatory signal. From River Hub's dry-spill detection over event-level data:

- **Rattery STW — the catchment's worst dry-weather spiller by a wide margin:** ~**4,040** dry-weather
  spill activations on record (next-worst ~580), and **still dry-spilling in 2026**. It is also the only
  top spiller under a mandated **improvement** order (`U_IMP1`) — not due until **2030**. Worst dry
  spiller + a fix a decade away while it keeps spilling = the single strongest case on the Dart.
- **Holne STW — the worst *right now*:** **8 of its 19** spills so far in 2026 were dry-weather (most of
  any Dart asset), yet it carries **monitoring/investigation WINEP only — no committed fix**.
- **Totnes STW** — 3 dry in 2026, 133 on record; monitoring only.
- **St Lukes Church CSO, Buckfastleigh** — **all 3** of its 2026 spills were dry; small but flagrant, no WINEP.

*Counts are event-level activations (more granular than the EA annual "spill count"), so absolutes run
higher than the annual totals in §1 — the point is the relative dry-weather burden and that it continues
in 2026. The equivalent River Teign briefing is in `TEIGN-SPILL-PRIORITY.md`.*
